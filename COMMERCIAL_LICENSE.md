## When do you need a commercial licence?

You need a commercial licence if you intend to:

- Deploy a customised or white-labelled version of FreeLingo for your organisation or your clients **without** publishing your modifications as open source.
- Offer FreeLingo as a managed service to third parties as part of a commercial product, without complying with the AGPL's source-disclosure requirements.
- Keep proprietary integrations, branding, or configuration changes private.

You do **not** need a commercial licence if you:

- Self-host an unmodified copy of FreeLingo for internal use (AGPL permits this).
- Use FreeLingo for personal, educational, or non-commercial purposes.
- Contribute improvements back to the project under the AGPL.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I evaluate FreeLingo before purchasing a commercial licence?**
Yes. You can run an unmodified copy locally or on a private server for evaluation purposes under the AGPL at no cost.

**Do I need a commercial licence just to use FreeLingo in my company?**
No — internal self-hosting of an unmodified copy is permitted under the AGPL without any additional licence.

**What happens if I modify FreeLingo and keep those changes private without a commercial licence?**
That would be a violation of the AGPL-3.0. The AGPL requires you to make the source code of modified versions available to users of the service.
